Governance

BYLAWS & VOTING

Transparent governance structures that ensure accountability and protect organizational integrity.

THREE VOTING CATEGORIES

Tiered decision-making for appropriate oversight

📝

ORDINARY

Simple Majority (50%+1)

Routine operational decisions that keep the organization running day-to-day. These require a simple majority of voting board members.

  • Practice schedule changes
  • Tournament selection and registration
  • Minor equipment purchases
  • Communication methods and timing
  • Event planning details
📈

SIGNIFICANT

Supermajority (2/3)

Important decisions that materially affect the organization's direction, budget, or operations. These require two-thirds approval.

  • Budget allocations over threshold
  • Policy changes and updates
  • Staff changes or additions
  • Partnership or sponsorship agreements
  • Program expansions
🔑

FOUNDATIONAL

Unanimous Consent

Core decisions that define the organization's identity, structure, or foundational documents. These require unanimous approval.

  • Bylaws amendments
  • Leadership structure changes
  • Mission or values modifications
  • Dissolution or merger decisions
  • Core governance changes

FINANCIAL AUTHORITY STRUCTURE

Tiered approval levels for financial decisions

$0-$250 per item
OPERATIONAL DISCRETION

Any Co-Director may approve. For routine operational needs, supplies, and minor equipment. Documented via receipt submission.

$251-$500 per item
CO-DIRECTOR APPROVAL

Requires approval from two Co-Directors. For significant purchases, planned expenses, and larger equipment items.

$501-$1,500 per item
BOARD VOTE

Requires simple majority board approval. For major equipment, uniforms, and significant program expenses.

$1,500+ per item
SUPERMAJORITY REQUIRED

Requires 2/3 board approval. For major investments, large purchases, and significant financial commitments.

COACHING INDEPENDENCE PROTECTION

Our bylaws include explicit protections to ensure coaching staff can focus entirely on player development without interference from administrative or financial pressures.

The Head Coach maintains full authority over all on-field decisions, player development strategies, and game-day management.

LINEUP AUTONOMY

Head Coach has sole authority over batting orders, defensive positions, and playing time decisions.

PRACTICE CONTROL

Coaching staff designs and executes all practice plans without board interference.

STRATEGY FREEDOM

Game strategy, signals, and tactical decisions rest entirely with coaching staff.

DEVELOPMENT PATH

Player development priorities and training focus determined by coaching expertise.
